The case for STR

Mutual respect, shared streets.

Walking, running and cycling are fast becoming popular activities in the country today — both for recreation and daily transportation. It has become imperative that our roads should be safe for all road users.

In order to co-exist safely, we need mutual respect and understanding between all road users, as they share the same rights and responsibilities on public roads.

Campaign Objectives

What STR aims to accomplish.

In alignment with the Non-Motorised Transport policy, the THEMES pillars for a "Greater Lagos", and the Sustainable Development Goals of the UN, the campaign aims to:

⌘

Educate

Educate all road users on their obligation to share the road.

⊕

Reinforce

Reinforce the use of the highway code.

♡

Encourage

Encourage road safety etiquette and courtesy between all users.

↺

Promote

Promote walking, running and cycling awareness — and the behavioural shift that makes it stick.

Tone of Campaign

Safety, taken seriously.

Safety is an integral part of this campaign and must be taken seriously. The safety of all road users — including pedestrians and cyclists — must be seen as paramount.

EMOTIONAL

Material that connects with people, not just informs them.

APPEALING

Designed and produced to be shared, not skipped.

DRAMATIC

Enough to draw attention to the issues — without trivialising them.

Infrastructure

From idle spaces to bicycle parks.

In conjunction with the African Conservation Foundation (ACF) and the Lagos State Parks and Gardens Agency (LASPARK), we plan to convert idle spaces — road and drainage setbacks — into safe, eco-friendly bicycle and recreational parks with cycle paths. These may eventually be incorporated into the city's cycling network.

The first of these bike parks is planned along Freedom Way in Lekki Phase 1.
