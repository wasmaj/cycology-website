Organizing Rides

How we group, partner up, and ride safely — plus the full weekly schedule and the No Helmet, No Ride policy that keeps everyone safe.

⌘

Groupings

Cyclists of similar ability ride together, with groups capped at 14 riders. Riders must choose appropriate groups matching their fitness on the day — to avoid faster riders being slowed down or slower riders struggling to hold the wheel.

⌥

The "Partner" System

Each rider must have a designated partner at ride start. Partners are responsible for ensuring their companion isn't left behind, with the peloton riding in pairs except when obstacles force single-file formation.

⌫

Bailing Out

Riders departing early should notify the ride captain — to prevent unnecessary search efforts and keep the group's focus on the road.

Weekly Schedule

Six rides a week. Pick your start time.

Our official Saturday club ride is the main event; the rest range from official to "you're on your own out there" unofficial.

Saturday · 6:30am · Official

Saturday Club Ride

The main event — departs at 6:30am from the Club House in Ikoyi. A ride captain is appointed daily to manage pace and route.

Tue & Thu · 8:30pm · Unofficial

Tuesday/Thursday Night Ride (TNR)

Departs 8:30pm. Unofficial status means cyclists provide their own support. Lights are mandatory.

Mon, Wed, Fri · 4:30am

Silly Hour Crew

Pre-dawn riding three times a week. Wake up before everyone else, log the miles, then head to work.

Sunday · 5:15am · Unofficial

Sunday Sunday Medicine (SSM)

Unofficial endurance ride for those chasing distance. Starts at 5:15am — and lives up to the name.

Sunday · all day

Full Ground Taya

Adventure rides on fat or mountain bikes, exploring the outskirts of Lagos via beaches and forest terrain. Full-day Sunday events.

Bi-monthly Saturday

Alternate Rides

Bi-monthly Saturday rides using off-road bikes on lesser-traveled routes.
